Navigating the OBBBA Tax Changes

This guide is designed for tax professionals to quickly understand and apply the significant tax law changes introduced by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). Use this resource to advise your individual and business clients effectively.

High-Level Tax Impact

The bill's tax provisions primarily consist of making the 2017 TCJA permanent, with new targeted (but temporary) relief measures. Read fiscal analysis →

Individual Tax Changes

Rates & Standard Deduction

OBBBA makes the individual tax rates and higher standard deduction from the TCJA permanent, preventing the scheduled "tax cliff" in 2026. The standard deduction is also increased beyond the TCJA levels.

New Targeted Deductions (Temporary)

The bill introduces three high-profile, temporary deductions aimed at middle-class taxpayers. These expire after 2028.

Estate & Gift Tax

The bill permanently sets the estate and gift tax exemption at a much higher level, preventing the scheduled drop.

Business Tax Changes

Pass-Throughs & Corporations

OBBBA provides permanent tax relief and certainty for both pass-through entities and C-corporations.

  • Sec. 199A QBI Deduction: The 20% deduction for Qualified Business Income from pass-throughs is made permanent.
  • Corporate Tax Rate: The 21% flat corporate tax rate is made permanent.

Practice Point: The permanence of the QBI deduction solidifies its importance in choice-of-entity analysis. Continue to evaluate S-Corp vs. C-Corp structures for clients, now with the certainty that these key provisions will remain.

Capital Investment & Expensing

The bill makes permanent two of the most powerful pro-growth provisions for business investment.

  • 100% Bonus Depreciation: The ability to immediately deduct 100% of the cost of eligible property (e.g., equipment, machinery) is made permanent, reversing the scheduled phase-down.
  • R&D Expensing: The requirement to amortize R&D expenses over 5 years is repealed. Businesses can once again immediately expense domestic R&D costs.

Planning Opportunity: Advise clients to re-evaluate their capital expenditure plans. The permanence of 100% bonus depreciation provides a powerful incentive to invest in new equipment and technology.

ACTION REQUIRED: Termination of Energy Credits

OBBBA terminates numerous clean energy credits. Clients who have made investment decisions based on these credits need immediate advice.

Urgent Practice Point: Identify all individual and business clients who may have planned to claim these credits. They may need to accelerate project timelines to place property in service before the deadlines. This is especially critical for the Clean Vehicle credit.
